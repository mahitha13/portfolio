iAdvisor

A website for planning the undergraduate four year roadmap

Team Size: 3 | Role: User Interviews, Ideating, Prototyping

_______________________________


The Client

UMD iSchool is rapidly growing each year and the undergraduate advisors are understaffed to deal with this number of students one on one to discuss their four year plan which is currently filled out on a pdf and manually verified by the advisors. To address this issue we have digitized the undergraduate roadmap. The website allows the student to fill in their preffered courses and automatically validates if there are any issues with plan like identifiying missing pre-requisites and exceeding credits.

The Process

Stakeholder Interviews

We identified stakeholders and conducted interviews with people from all the identified groups. The stakeholders for this project were the iSchool advisors, undergraduate students, faculty and staff. In the interviews we identified the issues amd needs the stakeholders have with respect to the undergraduate roadmap and started our brainstorming process to tackle these points.

About Me

Ever since I was a small child I have been interested in design. I picked up art at a very young age and even took drawing and painting lessons for over a year. Gradually as I grew up and got busy with my studies I lost touch with the creative side of me. I began taking interest in computers and ultimately pursued my formal college education in Computer Science for my Bachelor's degree. I was reunited with my love for design through my undergraduate studies when I took up Animation and Graphics in my 3rd semester. Finally, I was introduced to HCI in my final semester when I took up the course Designing Human Centered Systems at IIIT Delhi. I am forever grateful to my university and professors who introduced me to the field which allows me to explore my creativity and passion for computer interfaces.

My motivation to design not just as a hobby but serve a greater purpose in making people's life easier came from this course. To build my knowledge and be more informed about the field I decided to pursue my Master's in HCI from University of Maryland. I have never been more happier with a decision, as I have learnt a lot in my first semester here and have been thoroughly happy despite a busy schedule. My ideologies regarding the field have been shattered and reinvented and I am excited to learn more in the semesters to come. Till then I hope I get the oppurtunity to serve people's needs to the best of my knowledge and design solutions that are both visually pleasing and usable.
